Documenting my trip to Kauai !!

Kauai is an island in the Central Pacific, part of the Hawaiian archipelago. It's nicknamed "the Garden Isle" thanks to the tropical rainforest covering much of its surface. The dramatic cliffs and pinnacles of its Na Pali Coast have served as a backdrop for major Hollywood films, while 10-mile-long Waimea Canyon and the Nounou Trails traversing the Sleeping Giant mountain ridge are hiking destinations.

Short Intro:

Airports: Kauai’s main airport is Lihue Airport (LIH) in southeastern Lihue.
Kauai Resort Areas: There are five major resort destinations on Kauai: North Shore (Princeville), East Side (Coconut Coast),
Lihue (Kalapaki), South Shore (Poipu), West Side (Waimea).
Capital City: Lihue
Population:10000502 62,640
Time Zone: Hawaii Standard Time (GMT-10 hours), 5 hours behind the US East Coast,6hours behind during Daylight Saving Time
Languages: English Hawaiian
Currency: US dollar. Credit cards are widely accepted. Traveler’s checks are accepted at many businesses.

Napali Coast

Kauai’s Na Pali Coast is famous for its seaside beauty, marine life and water sports.The 15-mile length of coast is lined by cliffs, white-sand beaches and turquoise sea. biggest fun here is whale watch or spot dolphins on an eco-cruise or sailing adventure.some tourists were following the Kalalau Trail to go hiking across the cliff tops to Hanakapiai beach and waterfalls.
The Na Pali Coast is on Kauai’s north-west coast, protected by the Na Pali Coast State Park.

Smith Family Garden Luau and Boat ride

The torches are lit, the night is filled with music, and family members gather to bring us a casual, relaxing and romantic dining experience that starts off with the Imu Ceremony and thirst-quenching tropical m(c)ocktails are served inside the luau hale. The luau meal features traditional luau favorites, tropical fruit and a special Hawaiian dessert table Hawaiian musicians serenade dinners during the luau meal. Young members of the family take the stage during the entertainment, and even invite guests up on stage for a hula lesson! The International Pageant depicts dances and songs from Tahiti, Hawaii, China, Japan, the Philippines, New Zealand and Samoa.
